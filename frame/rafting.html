Rafting

Rafting in Nepal is one of the most thrilling and popular adventure activities, drawing adrenaline seekers from around the world. With its fast-flowing rivers originating from the Himalayas, Nepal offers some of the best white-water rafting experiences in the world. Rivers like the Trishuli, Bhote Koshi, Seti, Kali Gandaki, and Sun Koshi offer rapids ranging from gentle and beginner -friendly to intense Grade IV and V rapids for experienced rafters. Each river offers a unique experience, combining excitement with breathtaking scenery of hills, forests, and traditional villages along the banks.


Trishuli Rafting                  

Rafting trips in Nepal can be as short as a half-day adventure or extend to multi-day expeditions that include camping on riverbanks under the stars. These trips are perfect not just for adventure enthusiasts but also for those who want to enjoy nature in a fun and engaging way. Safety is a top priority, with trained guides, proper gear, and well-organized tour companies ensuring a smooth and exciting ride.            Bhote Koshi River

The Seti River is one of the best rivers in Nepal for a gentle and scenic rafting experience. It flows through beautiful valleys, lush forests, and remote villages, offering stunning views and a peaceful environment. The river has warm water and mostly easy rapids, making it perfect for beginners, families, and those looking for a relaxing adventure. The rafting trip usually lasts two days, with an overnight stay at a riverside camp, where you can enjoy nature and stargazing. The Seti River is also great for spotting birds and wildlife along the way. It's a wonderful choice if you want a mix of soft adventure and natural beauty in a short time.

Seti River Rafting                  

The Kali Gandaki River offers one of the most thrilling and scenic rafting experiences in Nepal. Flowing between the majestic Annapurna and Dhaulagiri mountain ranges, it runs through the world’s deepest gorge. The river is known for its exciting class III to IV rapids, making it a great choice for adventure seekers with some rafting experience.Along the way, you'll pass through remote villages, lush forests, waterfalls, and ancient temples, giving the trip a cultural touch as well. The rafting journey usually lasts 3 to 4 days, with camping on beautiful sandy beaches under the stars.


         Kali Gandaki River Rifting

The Sun Koshi River, meaning "River of Gold," is one of the best long-distance rafting rivers in the world. It offers an incredible multi-day adventure (usually 7 to 9 days), starting near the Tibetan border and flowing through remote valleys, dense forests, and traditional villages before joining the Ganges in India.This river features a thrilling mix of class III to V rapids, making it perfect for experienced rafters or adventurous beginners looking for a challenge. Along the way, you'll enjoy beautiful scenery, riverside camping, and a chance to disconnect from the busy world. The journey also includes calm sections for swimming, relaxing, and spotting wildlife.

Sun Koshi River Rifting             

The Karnali River is one of the longest and wildest rivers in Nepal, offering one of the most remote and adventurous rafting experiences in the country. Flowing from the Tibetan Plateau through deep gorges and pristine wilderness, the Karnali River provides a thrilling experience for experienced rafters. The river is known for its class IV to V rapids, which makes it ideal for those seeking an intense adventure and a true test of skill.The rafting journey on the Karnali typically lasts 7 to 10 days, and it takes you through rugged landscapes, stunning cliffs, dense forests, and remote villages. You'll also encounter isolated, peaceful areas that are perfect for camping along the riverbanks, where you can enjoy nature and star-filled skies.
