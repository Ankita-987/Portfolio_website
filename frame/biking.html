Biking

Mountain biking in Nepal is an exhilarating way to explore the country’s rugged beauty, diverse landscapes, and cultural richness. From the bustling streets of Kathmandu to the serene trails of the Annapurna region, biking offers a unique perspective that combines adventure with cultural immersion. Popular biking routes include the Kathmandu Valley loop, Pokhara to Jomsom, and the remote and arid trails of Upper Mustang, which resemble Tibetan landscapes. These routes take riders through traditional villages, terraced farmlands, dense forests, and breathtaking mountain scenery.


Upper and Lower Mustang     


Nepal’s varied terrain provides something for every level of rider—from easy countryside rides to technical off-road trails that challenge even experienced mountain bikers. Along the way, you can stop at ancient temples, interact with local communities, and enjoy panoramic views of the Himalayas. With a growing number of bike rental shops and guided tours available, mountain biking in Nepal is not only accessible but also one of the most immersive and exciting ways to experience the country.



       Annapurna Circuit

The Nagarkot to Bhaktapur biking route is a beautiful and scenic ride that takes you from the hill station of Nagarkot, known for its stunning views of the Himalayas, including Mount Everest, to the historic city of Bhaktapur. The journey begins with a downhill descent from Nagarkot, offering panoramic views of terraced fields, rural villages, and lush green landscapes. Along the way, you’ll pass through peaceful villages like Bikram, where you can enjoy the serene surroundings and interact with locals. The route ends in Bhaktapur, a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its ancient temples, narrow streets, and medieval architecture, particularly around Bhaktapur Durbar Square.

The Kathmandu Valley Rim biking route is a perfect way to explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage surrounding the Kathmandu Valley. This scenic ride takes you along the rim of the valley, offering stunning views of the Himalayas, lush forests, traditional villages, and ancient temples. The route is a mix of dirt and paved roads, making it suitable for bikers of various skill levels, from beginners to intermediate riders.Starting from Kathmandu, the ride often takes you to popular spots like Nagarkot, Kopan Hill, and Shivapuri National Park, offering peaceful trails, green forests, and breathtaking landscapes. .

The Chitwan to Lumbini biking route is a unique and rewarding journey through the heart of Nepal's Terai region, offering a mix of natural beauty, cultural exploration, and adventure. Starting from Chitwan, a popular destination known for its Chitwan National Park and wildlife safaris, the ride takes you through flat, open terrain, lush fields, and peaceful rural landscapes. The route is relatively flat, making it a good option for intermediate bikers, with a distance of approximately 150–200 km, typically spread over 2 to 3 days depending on your pace.Along the way, you’ll pass through small villages, farmland, and picturesque landscapes, providing an opportunity to experience the local way of life. The ride also offers a chance to spot wildlife in the surrounding areas, with the possibility of seeing animals like elephants, deer, and various bird species. .
