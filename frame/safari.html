Safari

A safari in Nepal is a unique and thrilling way to explore the country’s rich wildlife and natural beauty. Nepal’s national parks, including Chitwan National Park and Bardia National Park, are renowned for their biodiversity and provide excellent opportunities for wildlife enthusiasts to observe rare and endangered species in their natural habitats. Chitwan, located in the southern lowlands of Nepal, is one of the most famous safari destinations, known for its population of one-horned rhinoceroses, tigers, leopards, and various species of deer, as well as a rich variety of birdlife.


Chitwan National Park          


Safari activities include jeep safaris, which take you deep into the forest to spot wildlife from the comfort of a vehicle, as well as elephant safaris, where you can ride on the back of an elephant to explore the jungle. Canoeing along the Rapti River and nature walks with trained guides are also popular activities, offering a chance to get closer to nature and spot animals from a different perspective. For those interested in bird watching, Nepal's national parks are home to over 500 bird species, making them a haven for ornithologists. Whether you're on a family vacation, a wildlife photography trip, or just looking for a peaceful escape into nature, a safari in Nepal offers an unforgettable experience amidst lush jungles and the sounds of wildlife.

 Bardia National Park      

The Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve is a premier wildlife destination in eastern Nepal, renowned for its rich biodiversity and scenic landscapes. Located along the Sapta Koshi River, this reserve spans 175 square kilometers and is home to over 400 species of birds, including the Bengal Florican and Sarus Crane, making it a paradise for bird watchers. The reserve is also known for housing the endangered wild water buffalo, along with other wildlife such as Nilgai, golden jackals, and deer. Safari activities in Koshi Tappu include jeep safaris, which allow visitors to explore its diverse landscapes and spot wildlife, bird watching to observe a variety of avian species, and river rafting on the Sapta Koshi River, offering a unique perspective of the reserve’s wildlife.

Koshi Tappu Wildlife Reserve       

Parsa Wildlife Reserve, located in the southern Terai region of Nepal, offers a fantastic safari experience amidst diverse ecosystems, including subtropical Sal forests, grasslands, and the Churia Hills. Spanning across parts of Chitwan, Makawanpur, Parsa, and Bara districts, the reserve is home to a rich variety of wildlife, including the Royal Bengal Tiger, Wild Asian Elephant, Leopard, Sloth Bear, and Great Hornbill. Safari activities in Parsa include elephant safaris, where visitors can ride on the back of an elephant through the jungle to spot wildlife up close, jeep safaris for exploring the reserve's extensive trails, and nature walks guided by naturalists to learn about the flora and fauna.

Rara National Park, located in the remote northwestern region of Nepal, is a hidden gem known for its pristine beauty. Covering an area of 106 km², the park is home to the largest lake in Nepal, Rara Lake, situated at an altitude of 2,990 meters. Established in 1976, the park was created to conserve the unique landscapes and rich biodiversity of the Humla-Jumla region. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including endangered species such as the red panda, snow leopard, musk deer, and Himalayan black bear. It also supports over 200 bird species, including the vibrant Himalayan monal. Visitors to Rara National Park can enjoy activities such as trekking, boating, fishing, and wildlife viewing.
